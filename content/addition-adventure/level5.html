How to Solve Addition Word Problems

Follow these steps to solve any addition word problem:

1
Read the Problem Carefully

Understand what the problem is asking.

2
Find the Key Information

Look for the important numbers.

3
Decide What to Do

For addition problems, look for words like: sum, total, altogether, in all, combined

4
Write Your Number Sentence

Set up your addition problem.

5
Solve the Problem

Add the numbers and find the answer.

Example Problem #1

Ria collected 27 seashells at the beach. Her brother collected 18 more seashells. How many seashells do they have altogether?

Step 1: Read the Problem

We need to find the total number of seashells.

Step 2: Find Key Information

- Ria collected 27 seashells
- Her brother collected 18 seashells

Step 3: Decide What to Do

The word "altogether" tells us to add the numbers.

Step 4: Write Number Sentence

27 + 18 = ?

Step 5: Solve
27
+18
45

Answer: They have 45 seashells altogether.

Example Problem #2

The school library has 145 storybooks and 238 information books. How many books does the library have in total?

Step 1: Read the Problem

We need to find the total number of books in the library.

Step 2: Find Key Information

- 145 storybooks
- 238 information books

Step 3: Decide What to Do

The word "total" tells us to add the numbers.

Step 4: Write Number Sentence

145 + 238 = ?

Step 5: Solve
11
145
+238
383

Answer: The library has 383 books in total.

Tips for Solving Word Problems

  • Underline important numbers and information
  • Look for clue words that tell you to add:
    • in all
    • altogether
    • combined
    • total
    • sum
    • more
  • Draw a picture if it helps you understand the problem
  • Estimate to check if your answer makes sense
  • Always write a complete sentence for your answer

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Rushing and not reading the entire problem
  • Forgetting what the question is asking for
  • Using the wrong operation (addition vs. subtraction)
  • Not including the correct units in your answer (like books, apples, etc.)
