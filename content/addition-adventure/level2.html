Two-Digit Addition Basics

When adding a single digit to a two-digit number, we need to:

  1. Add the single digit to the ones place of the two-digit number
  2. If the sum is less than 10, we're done!
  3. If the sum is 10 or more, we carry the tens digit to the tens place
Example 1: Without carrying
24 + 5 = 29

Step 1: Add the ones digit (4) and 5: 4 + 5 = 9

Step 2: Keep the tens digit (2) as is

Result: 29

Example 2: With carrying
37 + 6 = 43

Step 1: Add the ones digit (7) and 6: 7 + 6 = 13

Step 2: Put 3 in the ones place and carry the 1 to the tens place

Step 3: Add the tens digit (3) and the carried 1: 3 + 1 = 4

Result: 43

Addition Tips

  • Focus on the ones place: When adding a single digit, only the ones place can change at first.
  • Watch for carrying: If adding to the ones place gives you 10 or more, don't forget to carry!
  • Mental math: Try to practice doing these additions in your head. Start with the ones place.
  • Real-world thinking: Think of adding objects, like having 35 marbles and getting 7 more.

Fun Math Facts!

  • The word "addition" comes from the Latin word "additio" which means "to add to".
  • Two-digit numbers were first used over 5,000 years ago in ancient Mesopotamia!
  • People count money every day using addition - especially when adding coins to dollars!
